How it works

How the sausage is made.

What this is

A hobby model that projects strikeout totals for every MLB starting pitcher, compares them to sportsbook over/under lines, and ranks any positive-expected-value edges. It runs itself: data refreshes several times a day, every projection is logged before games start, and results are reconciled automatically overnight. Nothing is ever deleted or cherry-picked — the Performance page is the whole record.

The model

A LightGBM gradient-boosted tree model with a Poisson objective produces the point estimate, and five separate quantile models (p10/p25/p50/p75/p90) trace the predictive distribution. A smooth monotone curve (PCHIP) through those quantiles gives the probability of clearing any line. It retrains weekly on an expanding window of every start since 2022.

Features, in order of importance

  • Pitcher swing-and-miss form: CSW% (called strikes + whiffs per pitch), SwStr%, and K% as exponentially-weighted averages with a half-life of ~5 starts. CSW% stabilizes much faster than K%, which is why it leads.
  • Opponent lineup K% vs handedness: slot-weighted strikeout rate of the nine projected hitters against the pitcher's hand, blending the last 30 days (60%) with the season (40%).
  • Expected workload: recent batters-faced average plus the game's run environment (Vegas total when available, otherwise both teams' recent scoring).
  • Stabilizers and context: K-BB%, fastball velocity trend (fatigue flag), park K factor, home-plate umpire tendency, temperature/wind for outdoor parks, home/away, days of rest.
  • Early-season prior: a Marcel-style regressed prior from last season, blended by sample size — the model leans on the prior in April and on current form by June (weight = BF / (BF + 70)).
  • Deliberately excluded: batter-vs-pitcher history (mostly noise under ~25 PA).

When lineups aren't posted yet

Lineup cards usually post 30–90 minutes before first pitch. Until then the model falls back: confirmed lineup → most-used hitters over the last 7 days → team-average K% vs hand. Each tier carries a lineup confidence (1.0 / 0.6 / 0.3) shown on every card — lower confidence widens the projected range and discounts the edge score rather than pretending certainty.

Edge math

For each entered line, the vig is stripped from the two-way price (multiplicative method), the model's probability is read off the projected distribution, and expected value per unit plus a quarter-Kelly stake are computed. Picks are ranked by EV × book weight × confidence weight. Only positive-EV sides are surfaced.

Honest limitations

  • K prop lines are entered manually (free APIs don't carry props), so coverage is whatever's typed in, when it's typed in.
  • Closing-line value is only measured when a closing line is also entered.
  • Retractable-roof parks: day-of roof status isn't freely available, so weather is attenuated rather than exact.
  • Trust the calibration plot and 30-day panel over any single hot streak — below roughly 500 settled picks, the hit rate is mostly noise either way.
